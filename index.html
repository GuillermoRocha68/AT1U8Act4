Activity 4

Grammar: Simple Past vs. Past Continuous

Simple Past: Used for completed actions in the past.

  • Affirmative: I walked to the park.
  • Negative: I didn't walk to the park.
  • Interrogative: Did you walk to the park?

Past Continuous: Used for ongoing actions in the past, often interrupted by a simple past action.

  • Affirmative: I was walking when it rained.
  • Negative: I wasn't walking very fast.
  • Interrogative: Were you walking alone?

Activity 7

Grammar: WHEN vs. WHILE

WHEN is usually followed by a short action in the Simple Past.

Example: I was reading a book when the phone rang.

WHILE is usually followed by a continuous action in the Past Continuous.

Example: While I was reading a book, the phone rang.
